## 1. Positioning

**The brand exists because trust in Indian financial advice is broken.**

Most Indians and NRIs find their financial advisor through a referral from family, a friend at work, or a relationship manager at their bank. The advisor is rarely the best fit — they are simply the closest fit by accident of social proximity. Bloomkite replaces that accident with structure. Every advisor is verified, credentialed, and reviewable before any conversation begins.

### Positioning statement

> For **Indian and NRI investors** who want a financial advisor they can trust on merit rather than memory, **Bloomkite is a verified advisor marketplace** that makes credentials, track records, and specialisations transparent before the first conversation. Unlike informal referral networks, bank relationship managers, and self-service investing apps, **Bloomkite is the only place where the trust layer is the product itself**.

### What Bloomkite is NOT

- Not a robo-advisor (Bloomkite does not give financial advice)
- Not a self-service investing app (not Groww, INDmoney, Zerodha, Coin)
- Not a wealth management firm (does not manage money or hold assets)
- Not an unfiltered directory (curated; advisors verified before they appear)

### Tagline candidates

| | Tagline | Use |
|---|---|---|
| A | Find an advisor on merit, not memory. | Direct, names the problem |
| **B (Recommended)** | **Verified advisors. Honest choices.** | Two-part rhythm, product + benefit |
| C | Trust, before you talk. | Most distinctive, abstract |

## 2. Brand promise

> **Verified advisors. Real credentials, real track records, real choice.**

Bloomkite makes it possible to choose a financial advisor the way you'd choose a doctor or a lawyer: by qualification, evidence, and fit, not by who happens to call your family.

### Three principles

1. **Verification is the product.** Every surface reinforces that every advisor has passed a real bar. The verified state is never decorative.
2. **Clarity over enthusiasm.** Hype destroys financial trust faster than it builds it. The brand never sells what it can't prove.
3. **Respect the weight of the decision.** Investors come with real money and real anxiety. The brand never trivialises the stakes.

## 3. Audience model

Three primary audiences, each a first-class brand surface.

### A — The Indian investor
- Salaried, business owner, or retired. 28–65. Urban and tier-2.
- Has outgrown DIY investing or never wanted it. Wants a human advisor but doesn't trust the bank RM and doesn't know how to evaluate independent advisors. Has been burned, or knows someone who has.
- **Needs from the brand**: credibility signals that feel earned, not corporate. Plain language. Evidence over assertion.

### B — The credentialed advisor
- SEBI RIA, CFP, CA, or NISM-certified. 5+ years of practice.
- Has a small but loyal client base built through referrals. Wants to grow but is uncomfortable with marketing. Refuses to compromise on fiduciary standards. Worries that listing on a platform will commoditise their service.
- **Needs from the brand**: a platform that elevates their credentials, not dilutes them. Premium presentation. Discretion in lead handling.

### C — The NRI investor (strategic wedge)
- 28–55. US, UK, UAE, Singapore, Canada, Australia. Earning in foreign currency, investing in India.
- NRE/NRO accounts. Indian mutual funds, real estate, sometimes direct equity. FEMA, DTAA, TDS, repatriation complexity. Highest willingness to pay for the right advisor. Lowest tolerance for fumbled basics.
- **Needs from the brand**: proof the platform understands their specific complexity. Time-zone awareness. Multi-currency context. Explicit NRI specialisation tags on advisor profiles.
- **This audience is the strategic wedge for launch.**

## 4. Voice and tone

Three words: **Authoritative · Plain · Considered**.

### Voice in practice

| Write | Avoid |
|---|---|
| "Rajesh Kumar is a SEBI-registered investment advisor with 14 years of practice. He specialises in NRI taxation and retirement planning. His client portfolios have returned a median 11.2% over the last 5 years." | "Rajesh is a superstar advisor who will transform your wealth journey with expert guidance and a proven track record of excellence!" |
| "We verify every advisor's credentials before they appear on Bloomkite. You see the result of that verification on every profile." | "Our AI-powered platform uses cutting-edge algorithms to match you with the perfect financial advisor for your unique needs." |
| "NRI investing in India is complicated. Finding an advisor who actually understands FEMA, repatriation, and DTAA shouldn't be." | "Unlock your India investment potential with our revolutionary NRI advisor discovery platform!" |
| "Bloomkite does not give financial advice. We connect you with advisors who do. The decision, and the responsibility, stays with you and your advisor." | "Bloomkite is your trusted partner in achieving your financial dreams!" |

### Regulatory voice rule

Bloomkite is not a SEBI-registered investment advisor. Bloomkite never recommends specific advisors, securities, or strategies. Disclaimer text is written in plain language, not legal boilerplate, and is treated as part of the brand surface, not a footer afterthought. Every disclaimer is signed off by the business owner before going live.

## 6. Typography

One distinctive serif. One disciplined sans. One mono for numbers. One serif for Devanagari.

| Family | Use | Font stack |
|---|---|---|
| **Fraunces** | Display, editorial, headings | `'Fraunces', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif` |
| **Inter** | Body, UI, navigation, forms | `'Inter', system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif` |
| **JetBrains Mono** | Numbers and financial data | `'JetBrains Mono', 'SF Mono', Consolas, monospace` |
| **Noto Serif Devanagari** | Hindi content | `'Noto Serif Devanagari', serif` |

Fraunces has variable optical sizing; use `font-variation-settings: "opsz" 144` for display and `opsz 9` for body italic.

JetBrains Mono is used exclusively for financial figures — currency, percentages, dates. Tabular alignment non-negotiable. Currency symbol always in the same weight as the figure.

## 7. Iconography

**Outline icons. 1.5px stroke. Rounded terminals.**

- Library: Phosphor Icons (Tabler as fallback).
- 24px default size, 20px for small UI density.
- Stroke width increases at small sizes (16px → 2.5px) to maintain definition.
- **Filled icons are reserved exclusively for the verification badge.** This single exception makes the verified state instantly readable across the entire system.
- The verified shield+tick mark is filled in Verified Green when on advisor profiles; white-on-Verified when used as a badge.

## 8. NRI surface (strategic wedge)

The NRI experience is a first-class brand surface, not a feature toggle.

### Required NRI features

- **Multi-currency display.** Advisor fees in INR with resident-country currency in parentheses.
- **Time-zone aware copy.** "Available for calls between 6pm and 10pm IST (8am–12pm EST)." Both zones shown automatically.
- **NRI specialisation tags.** Advisor profiles surface explicit tags: FEMA, DTAA US, DTAA UK, repatriation, NRE/NRO planning, GIFT City products.
- **Country-of-residence context.** Onboarding asks where the investor lives, then filters advisor results to those with relevant cross-border experience.

### NRI tone rule

The NRI surface does not patronise. It does not explain India to NRIs. It explains the Indian advisor market specifically, on the assumption that the user understands India culturally but needs help navigating the professional landscape. The voice is slightly more direct and less ceremonial than the domestic surface.
